📄 Reading Passage

The Rise of Remote Work

The COVID-19 pandemic fundamentally transformed the nature of work across the globe. Millions of employees who had previously commuted to offices found themselves working from home — a shift that, for many, became permanent even after restrictions lifted.

A 2023 survey by the International Labour Organization found that approximately 18% of global workers now work remotely at least part of the time, a figure that would have seemed extraordinary just a decade ago. Technology infrastructure — including high-speed internet, cloud computing, and video conferencing platforms — has been the backbone of this transition.

However, remote work is not without its critics. Research published in the Harvard Business Review suggests that employees working from home can experience feelings of isolation and reduced sense of belonging to their organizations. Collaboration on complex projects, some argue, remains most effective in person.

Economists debate whether remote work increases or decreases productivity. While some studies show a 13% productivity boost for remote call-center workers, others highlight the challenges of home environments with children, noise, and limited workspace.

Question 1
According to the passage, what percentage of global workers work remotely at least part of the time?
Question 2

Question 3
What productivity increase was noted for remote call-center workers in some studies?
